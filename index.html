ABOUT THE DH PROJECT

The first archived instance of the Marist Circle dates back to February 12, 1965.

There have been many changes over the newspaper’s 76 years. However, its mission has remained constant: the circle has been – and always will be – a newspaper for the students, by the students.

This DH project seeks to recognize and denote sentiment patterns between weekly/monthly and yearly editions of the first 3 years of the archived Marist Circle.


UNDERSTANDING SENTIMENT ANALYSIS

Sentiment analysis is the process of determining the attitude or the emotion of a piece of content, i.e., whether it is positive or negative or neutral.

The graph below displays the sentiment score for each student newspaper over the course of its inaugural three years. Sentiment score is a value between [-1, 1] where 1 is most positive and -1 is most negative.

The slider at the top of the graph denotes a threshold (minimum) subjectivity score. Subjectivity scores are provided with sentiment scores and are used to gauge how subjective (opinionated) or objective (factual) a piece of content is. Moving the slider to the right, increases the threshold and will limit the results on the graph to only show those that match that level of subjectivity.

WHAT CAN WE LEARN FROM THIS?

Looking over the data presented in the graph, we can see that there is one newspaper that has a significantly lower sentiment score than the rest, Vol. 4 Num. 2: October 19, 1967. Why is this?

The Oct 19, 1967 edition of the circle was uncharacteristically politcally charged. The edition contained articles that discussed topics such as: FDR's policies, the supreme court, the vietnam war, and addiction.

With these articles in mind, it is easy to see how this edition of the circle was the least positively charged edition in the circle's first three years.
